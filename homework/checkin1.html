What's Speleology?

Speleology is the study of caves, from mapping and recording passages to understanding their formation and even unique biology. Underground explorations can range from a few hours to months, and require a great deal of preparation.

Why go underground?

  1. It's the last terrestrial frontier that we have to explore in-person! Due to their hostile environments, it is nearly impossible to access caves remotely.
  2. Caves often host endangered, rare, or unique species of animals, especially in the United States.
  3. Advancements in technology in caving often can be applied to a host of different fields, as expeditions often must prepare for long-term isolated survival, underwater diving, and living in total darkness.
  4. It's fun (if you like being in small spaces) and great exercise. More importantly, the community is very tight-knit and excited to have new members.

How do I get involved?

  • The National Speleological Society has clubs called "grottos" nationwide who host community events and make caving accessible.
  • Because there are risks associated with caving that are not common in other outdoor sports and it is difficult to self-rescue, access to caves and systems is often limited. Groups and clubs are often the best, if not only, way to gain access.
